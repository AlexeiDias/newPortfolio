About me

I started my journey in computing when I was 13 years old. Back then, computers were very slow, and the screen interaction was not so attractive either. Not that exciting at first, but the fact that I could give instructions to a machine and the machine could respond, fascinated me.

Further then, in 1997, I graduated from a college that focused on data processing, which gave me more knowledge and experience. I then became a computer instructor at the same high school I attended. At the time we used the early versions of Windows and DOS systems. The commonly studied languages were PASCAL, VISUAL BASIC and DELPHI.

Looking to pursue further knowledge and skills, In 1999 my life took a different direction when I moved to the US. I came to California as a foreign student, to learn ESL and get a new cultural experience and overview of the world. That set me away from tech-related jobs for a while, but I never stopped homeschooling myself on the subject, not as often as I liked, but always doing my best to learn more.

Finally, after a few attempts trying new endevors, I took a decision to go back doing what I always loved, by attending a well-recognized silicon valley coding Bootcamp at UC Berkeley Extention. And that's how I stepped back on the tech field, learning the most up to date full-stack development technologies. Now I'm back and I'm ready to fly.
